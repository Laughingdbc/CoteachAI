ELA Prompts (K-2)

Sight Word Activities

T: Create 5 engaging, hands-on activities to help kindergarten students practice these sight words: [list 5-10 sight words].
E: Each activity should be similar to this example: "Sight Word Hopscotch - Draw a hopscotch grid with sight words in each square. Students say the word as they hop on it."
A: These activities are for kindergarten students who are visual and kinesthetic learners beginning to develop their sight word recognition.
C: We have limited classroom space but access to basic materials like paper, markers, index cards, and tape. Students know about half of the words already but need practice with quick recognition.
H: Format each activity with a clear title, materials needed, step-by-step instructions, and a brief note on how to make it easier or more challenging depending on student ability.

Phonics Lesson Plan

T: Design a complete 30-minute phonics lesson teaching the [specific sound pattern, e.g., short 'a' sound, consonant blends, etc.].
E: Structure the lesson following the gradual release model: "I do" (teacher modeling), "We do" (guided practice), and "You do" (independent practice).
A: This lesson is for 1st grade students who have mastered letter sounds but are still developing phonemic awareness skills.
C: We follow a systematic phonics program but need supplemental lessons to reinforce specific patterns. Several students are struggling with blending sounds into words.
H: Format the lesson with clear time allocations for each section, a list of materials needed, explicit teacher language for modeling, suggested word lists for practice, and a quick formative assessment activity.

Reading Comprehension Activities

T: Create 4 interactive reading comprehension activities focused on [specific skill: main idea, character traits, sequencing, etc.] that can be used with any picture book.
E: Activities should be similar to: "Story Map Characters - Students draw the main character and add descriptive words around them based on story evidence."
A: These activities are for 2nd grade students who can read simple texts independently but need support with deeper comprehension.
C: We do daily read-alouds and guided reading. Students are engaged during reading but struggle to discuss or respond to the text afterward.
H: Format each activity with an engaging title, clear learning objective, materials list, step-by-step instructions, and sample questions to guide student thinking.

Math Prompts (K-2)

Number Sense Activities

T: Design 5 hands-on math center activities to develop number sense for numbers [specific range, e.g., 1-20, 1-100].
E: Activities should be similar to: "Number Bond Cards - Students use manipulatives to find different ways to make a number and record their findings."
A: These activities are for kindergarten students with varying levels of number recognition and counting skills.
C: We have access to counters, ten frames, number cards, and basic art supplies. Students rotate through centers in 15-minute blocks with minimal teacher supervision.
H: Format each activity with a title, learning objective, materials list, setup instructions, student directions (simple enough for non-readers to follow with visual supports), and modification suggestions for different ability levels.

Addition & Subtraction Word Problems

T: Create 10 engaging word problems that practice addition and subtraction within [number range, e.g., 20, 100].
E: Problems should include various problem types like: "Sara had 8 stickers. Then her friend gave her 5 more stickers. How many stickers does Sara have now?"
A: These problems are for 1st-2nd grade students who understand the basic operations but need practice applying them to real situations.
C: Students have been taught addition and subtraction strategies including counting on, making ten, and using number lines. They need experience with different problem structures (add to, take from, compare, etc.).
H: Format as a printable worksheet with problems numbered and organized from simpler to more complex. Include space for drawing models, showing work, and writing answers. Add a teacher answer key with problem type identified for each question.

2D Shapes Lesson Plan

T: Design a complete lesson plan for teaching and exploring 2D shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle, hexagon).
E: Structure the lesson with a hook activity, direct instruction, guided exploration, independent practice, and formative assessment.
A: This lesson is for 1st grade students who need to identify shapes regardless of orientation or size, and describe their attributes.
C: We have pattern blocks, shape templates, and access to a document camera. Students have previously learned basic shape names but confuse shapes when they're rotated or sized differently.
H: Format the lesson plan with clear timing for each section, materials needed, specific questions to ask students, descriptions of activities, and a simple exit ticket to check understanding.

Science Prompts (K-2)

Animal Adaptations Lesson

T: Create a complete science lesson about animal adaptations focusing on how animals' physical features help them survive in their environments.
E: Structure the lesson with an engaging opening (animal guessing game), followed by clear explanations, visual examples, a hands-on activity, and a reflection/assessment component.
A: This lesson is for 2nd grade students who are curious about animals but have limited understanding of adaptation concepts.
C: We have access to pictures of animals, art supplies, and a classroom with space for movement. This lesson supports our unit on habitats and aligns with NGSS standards for biological evolution.
H: Format the lesson plan with clearly labeled sections for objectives, materials, vocabulary, procedure (timed steps), extensions for advanced learners, scaffolds for struggling students, and an assessment component.
